How to Connect your Laptop to your TV

Objective

To guide you through the process of connecting your laptop to your TV, both via HDMI cable and wirelessly.

Prerequisites

  • Laptop with an HDMI output or Wi-Fi capability.
  • TV with an HDMI input or Wi-Fi capability.
  • HDMI cable (if using Option A).
  • Wi-Fi network (if using Option B).
  1. Plug one end of the HDMI cable into the HDMI output on your laptop.
  2. Plug the other end of the cable into one of the HDMI inputs on your TV.
  3. Using the TV remote control, select the input that corresponds to where you plugged in the cable (HDMI 1, HDMI 2, HDMI 3, etc.).
  1. Make sure your laptop and TV are both connected to Wi-Fi.
  2. On your laptop, click the start button (bottom left corner of your screen).
  3. Type Settings on the search bar.
  4. Then, click System > Display.
  5. Under the Multiple displays header, click Connect to a wireless display. You should see a list of available displays.
  6. When your TV appears in the list, click on it to Connect your laptop.
  7. If you don’t see your TV on the list, click Detect under the Multiple displays header. If it still doesn’t appear, your TV or laptop may not support Miracast®, the technology that enables the devices to connect wirelessly. However, you may be able to connect your laptop to a streaming device, such as a Roku® streaming device, which you could then use to project the display onto your TV.

Process Complete

Your laptop is now connected to your TV!
